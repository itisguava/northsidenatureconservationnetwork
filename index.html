Nature can't protect itself.

It needs you.

We are a diverse group of citizens united by their commitment to seek greater protections for the ACT's ecological communities. Our aim is to foster community concern for the environment through site-specific events, with a focus on threatened and vulnerable native animals.

Our work

  • Dingoes in Namadji National Park with UNSW conservation scientists
  • Flying foxes, in collaboration with ACT Wildlife
  • Quoll spotlighting night-walks with community group See-Change
  • Chytrid fungus remedies and frog saunas with Frog Watch
  • A walk-and-talk, searching for longnecked turtles with Landcare ACT

How can you help?

  • Join the Northside Nature Conservation Network
  • Attend our events
  • Join our citizen science studies
  • Learn about the ACT's conservation issues
  • Lobby our lawmakers for stronger nature protection
  • Advocate for our diminishing biodiversity
